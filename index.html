About Me

My name is Matthew Amorelli, and I am a Computer Science and Engineering student at the University of Nevada, Reno pursuing a minor in Cyber Security. My primary interests are embedded systems and cybersecurity, particularly where software interacts directly with hardware and secure system design. Through my coursework and personal projects, I have developed experience building microcontroller-based systems using platforms such as ESP32 and Arduino, working with embedded C/C++, real-time operating systems, and hardware communication protocols. I enjoy designing efficient, reliable systems while also understanding how they can be secured against potential vulnerabilities. My portfolio showcases some of the projects I have developed as I continue expanding my skills in embedded development, low-level programming, and cybersecurity.
